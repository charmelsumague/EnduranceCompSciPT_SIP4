French Inventor and Chemist, Jacques Charles.

Jacques Alexandre Cesar Charles, more commonly known as Jacques Charles was a French scientist/chemist who was well known for his discovery of the relationship between the temperature and volume of a gas. Born on November 12th, 1746 and died on the 17th of April 1823. During his time, Charles had also invented the first hydrogen balloon along with his other notable discoveries.

More about: Charles's Law

Charles's Law states that the volume and temperature of a gas are directly proportionate, whilst pressure stays constant.

Formula: V1 / T1 = V2 / T2

  • V1 is the initial volume
  • T1 is the initial temperature
  • V2 is the final volume
  • T2 is the final temperature

    • Real Life Application

    A great example of Charles's Law at work is the hot air balloon. The fire heats up the molecules inside the balloon, causing them to move around more, expanding the volume of the balloon itself.

    Seatwork:

    A gas occupies a container that is 50L with its initial temperature being 23 degrees celsius. After the gas is heated, it increases to 42 degrees celsius.

    Solution

    The initial volume = 50L
    The initial temperature = 23 degrees celsius
    The final temperature = 42 degrees celsius
    Required: V2, Final volume.
    Note: In examples using Charles's Law, it is required to convert into kelvin. It is done by adding 273 to your initial and final temperature. To convert from kelvin to celsius, you subtract.
